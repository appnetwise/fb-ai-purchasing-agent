Page 3 — 1. Problem and Opportunity 

Restaurants today often run procurement and operations through fragmented channels (WhatsApp, calls, emails), which creates errors, delays, weak price transparency, and margin leakage, duplication invoices  

 
Suppliers struggle with inconsistent order formats, slow confirmations, limited demand visibility, and payment friction. 

AI Purchase Manager solves this by turning procurement into a controlled workflow and adding an AI layer that automates routine decisions while keeping humans in approval control. 

Core pain points (restaurant side) 

Scattered supplier lists and manual ordering cause wrong items, missing items, and delays. 

Prices cannot be compared fairly because items differ by naming, unit, and pack size. 

Manual back-office work: LPOs, invoice matching, approvals, chasing deliveries. 

Inventory surprises (stockouts) and overbuying increase spoilage and waste. 

Limited visibility into waste, variance, recipe inefficiencies, and true food cost. 

Delivery tracking  

Core pain points (supplier side) 

Orders arrive through multiple channels, increasing mistakes and response time. 

No unified dashboard of sales, customers, route performance, and product trends. 

No commission structure for the sales representative  

Inventory planning is difficult without demand forecasting. 

Promotion is running manually and through WhatsApp groups  

Payment delays and cashflow variability hurt SMEs. 

2. Solution Overview (Closed-Loop Platform) 

AI Purchase Manager is a full closed-loop operating system: 
Discover → Compare → Order → Track → Receive → Invoice → Pay/Credit → Inventory depletion → Low stock → AI drafts next order → Manager approves → Waste insights → Forecast and planning 

List/Promote → Identify target restaurants → Outreach (human-approved) → Quote/RFQ response → Convert to first order → Repeat ordering nudges → Promotions → Upsell/cross-sell → Commission payout 

North Star outcome 
Reduce kitchen and back-office workload while lowering food cost via autonomous purchasing and waste intelligence, without sacrificing control or compliance.

5. AI Layer: Where AI Reduces Operations 

AI is implemented as four practical engines. Start with rules and heuristics, then evolve to ML once data is clean. 

Engine 1: SKU Normalization and Comparable Pricing (Moat) 

Parse supplier catalogs, invoices, and POs into Normalized SKU objects 

Convert units and pack sizes to comparable unit economics 

Build equivalency sets (same item, different pack; approved substitutes) 

Outcome: true price comparison and fewer ordering errors 

Engine 2: AI Autopilot Purchasing (AI Suggested Cart) 

Predict run-rate per item using sales/consumption trends and lead times 

Generate a suggested cart per branch (days of cover + safety stock) 

Recommend supplier per item using price + reliability + lead time 

Approval workflow: AI drafts, Ops Manager edits/approves, PO created 

Learning loop: capture manager edits as feedback 

Engine 3: Waste Intelligence + Corrective Actions 

Detect anomalies (variance spikes, unusual waste patterns) 

Root-cause hints: over-portioning, spoilage, receiving errors, spec drift 

Recommend actions: prep plan changes, recipe substitutions, supplier swaps, promotions 

Optional: internal branch transfer suggestions for near-expiry items 

Engine 4: Kitchen Copilot (Daily Operations) 

Auto-generate prep lists based on forecast + current stock + expiry priorities 

Batch cooking suggestions and FEFO/FIFO prompts 

Surplus-based recipe ideas (chef approves) 

Supplier AI (to support suppliers) 

Sales dashboards and forecasts by customer, category, and route 

Item list enhancement: when the customer enters the items list (RFQ/purchase request), they can also add consumption (if available) and the target price required per item. 

Slow-moving stock alerts and recommended promotions 

Customer churn risk alerts (ordering frequency drops) 

Route consolidation and dispatch insights

Payment & credit 
Payment is bank transfer / cash / cheque / post-dated. Credit terms exist but tracking is manual. 

Inventory & waste 
Inventory counts happen weekly/monthly; waste is logged inconsistently (if at all). 
So “true food cost” and variance are often unknown until it’s too late.

Appendix: Demo Access, KYC, Item Inputs, and Sales Executive Attribution 

A1. 72-Hour Demo Access (Username + Password Only) 

Objective: Allow a restaurant/business owner to explore all platform features using a time-limited demo account (valid for 72 hours) with virtual items and virtual prices. 

Sequence 

1. Owner creates demo credentials (username + password). Optional: business name, owner name, email/phone. 

2. System provisions a demo tenant (demo restaurant workspace) and loads a demo dataset: virtual catalog (SKUs), virtual suppliers, and virtual pricing (optional sample history/analytics). 

3. User can navigate all modules and simulate RFQs/orders. Any submit action is simulation only (no messages sent to suppliers; no real transactions). 

4. Demo expires 72 hours from creation. After expiry the account is locked (or read-only) with a prompt to convert to live. 

Rules 

• All demo data must be isolated from live tenants and clearly labeled as Virtual/Demo. 

• Demo mode must not allow live supplier communication, real ordering, invoicing, payments, or credit features. 

A2. KYC Flow (Conversion from Demo to Live) 

Objective: Verify the restaurant/business identity and authorized signatory before enabling live transactions. 

KYC statuses and access 

• Demo (No KYC): Full navigation with virtual data; simulation only. 

• KYC Started/Pending: Drafting and setup allowed; live sending/ordering can be restricted until approval (configurable policy). 

• KYC Approved: Full live access. 

• KYC Rejected/Expired: Live actions restricted; user prompted to resubmit. 

Step-by-step sequence 

1. Trigger: KYC starts when the user selects Convert to Live or Enable Live Ordering. 

2. Business details: legal name, trade license number, address/emirate, VAT/TRN (if applicable), authorized signatory (name, mobile, email). 

3. Document upload (minimum set): Trade License (valid), Emirates ID/Passport (authorized signatory), VAT certificate (if applicable). Optional: proof of address, bank letter/IBAN, MOA (as required). 

4. Verification: automated checks (optional) + manual review by admin/compliance. 

5. Decision: Approved / Needs More Info / Rejected (include reason codes and reviewer notes). 

6. Renewal: trade license expiry reminders; re-KYC/renewal flow where required. 

Security and audit 

• Store KYC documents encrypted at rest. 

• Log every access to KYC documents (who viewed, when). 

• Apply a retention policy based on compliance requirements. 

A3. Item List Inputs Update (Consumption + Target Price) 

Requirement: When the customer enters the items list (RFQ / purchase request / basket), they can also add consumption (if available) and the target price required per item. 

Behavior 

• Each line item supports: item name/SKU, unit, quantity, consumption (optional), and target price (optional). 

• Quote comparison shows supplier quoted price vs target price, variance (amount/%), and a meets-target indicator. 

A4. Sales Executive Mapping + Target Attribution 

Objective: Link each restaurant to the relevant supplier/territory sales representative so sales are attributed to that representative and reflected in their targets. 

Relationship model 

• Preferred: Restaurant + Supplier -> Assigned Sales Executive (recommended to support different reps per supplier). 

• Alternative: Restaurant -> Default Sales Executive (if the business uses one rep for the territory across suppliers). 

Attribution rule 

• Every RFQ, quote, order, and confirmed purchase stores RestaurantID, SupplierID, and SalesExecID (from mapping). 

• Sales reporting and target tracking roll up by SalesExecID (by period, supplier, and restaurant). 

Contact surface (optional) 

• Restaurant profile displays assigned sales executive contact details (phone/WhatsApp). 

• Optional notifications to sales executive for new RFQs/orders in their accounts.
